Scraped data from https://huggingface.co/datasets/jamescalam/ai-arxiv2. The ai-arxiv2 dataset contains a selection of papers on the topics of AI and LLMs (arxiv.org).

Chunking Methodology:

Academic research papers, characterized by their extensive length and complex formatting, pose a challenge for machine readability. To address this, we implemented three distinct chunking strategies, customizing each to accommodate the variable structure of academic texts. Each chunk has overlap to help with continuity. This approach ensures the creation of more accessible, machine-readable segments, crucial for LLM processing.

  • 100-token chunks have a 15-token overlap.
  • 200-token chunks have a 20-token overlap.
  • 300-token chunks have a 25-token overlap.
  • 400-token chunks have a 30-token overlap.
Embedding Options:

Two embedding options are provided, varying according to the selected chunk size:

  1. Embed X Document Chunks: Aggregates the top X chunk results across multiple documents, facilitating a broad yet relevant context.
  2. Embed 1 Chunk with X Additional Context: Focuses on a single document source, embedding the matched chunk along with surrounding chunks to enrich the LLM's contextual understanding.
User Guidance:

Participants are encouraged to experiment with the available chunking and embedding configurations to identify optimal settings for specific use cases. Our goal is give users hands-on experience in order to spur insights on how to tailor embedded data - optimizing the balance between granularity, utility and contextual breadth.

Data Source & Version:

We utilized the Basic English Version of the Bible, made available through this GitHub repository, for our demo. This version is particularly suited for clear comprehension and accessibility, making it an ideal candidate for our textual analysis and retrieval pipelines.

Data Structuring Approach:

The JSON data structure provided the Bible text in two distinct segments: chapters and verses. In our initial example, we opted to retain these divisions, recognizing the inherent value in the traditional formatting. Given the widespread study and citation of Bible verses and chapters, altering this familiar structure could detract from the user experience without offering significant benefits in granularity. Our approach underscores the importance of tailoring data chunking techniques to the nature of the content, ensuring that both integrity and practicality are maintained.

Retrieval Pipelines & Prompt Templates:

Our demo showcases four retrieval pipelines, offering users a range of options for embedding text based on their query specifics.

  • Basic RAG Pipeline: Direct embedding of matched chunks - chapters or verses - allows for straightforward retrieval based on user queries. This simplicity serves well in maintaining textual coherence and relevance.
  • Small-to-Big Contextualization: This advanced technique enhances the granularity of searches by returning a larger contextual segment around the matched chunk. For instance, querying a verse can embed the entire chapter or surrounding verses, providing a richer context.

Additionally, we've made prompt templates to inspire creative applications of Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG). Among the templates, the political correctness rating system stands out. It evaluates the embedded text, offering a political correctness score for each segment. This application demonstrates the potential of LLM in assisting decision-making processes, showcasing the versatility of RAG beyond the demo's primary context.

Preparing data for embed
 

Our demo utilizes the COVID-19 dataset from Science.org (https://www.science.org/collections/coronavirus) , featuring 388 documents. This dataset serves as the foundation for demonstrating our retrieval augmented generation capabilities.

Data Preparation and Processing:
  1. Document Repository to Spreadsheet: Transfer document URLs to a spreadsheet and configure metadata, including title, type (report, research article, etc.), PDF URLs for scraping, reference URLs for lookups, and cover dates.
  2. Export to CSV: Configured metadata is exported from the spreadsheet to a CSV file for integration with the next stage.
  3. Import into Unacog Embedding: The CSV file is imported into Unacog Embedding, transitioning to data processing.
  4. Scraping: We prioritized scraping content from PDF sources over website URLs to ensure the highest data quality. During this phase, our UI detected and flagged errors related to inaccessible URLs. These are often due to paywall restrictions, leading to their exclusion from the dataset to maintain both accessibility and usability. This selective process ensures that only fully accessible documents are included, enhancing the overall quality and reliability of our data.
  5. Chunking: Content was segmented into 300-token chunks with a 25-token overlap

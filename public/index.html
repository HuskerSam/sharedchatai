AI Demo Brief: Strategic Chunking for LLM Contextual Understanding

Source and Dataset Overview:
  • AI Research Paper Source: arxiv.org
  • Approximate Cutoff Date: 07-18-2023
  • Document Count: 428
  • Dataset Utilized: Hugging Face Dataset jamescalam/ai-arxiv
Chunking Methodology:

Academic research papers, characterized by their extensive length and complex formatting, pose a challenge for machine readability. To address this, we implemented three distinct chunking strategies, customizing each to accommodate the variable structure of academic texts. This approach ensures the creation of more accessible, machine-readable segments, crucial for LLM processing.

Chunk Sizes:100 tokens, 200 tokens, and 400 tokens

Overlapping Tokens: Tailored to chunk size to maintain continuity:

  • 100-token chunks have a 15-token overlap.
  • 200-token chunks have a 20-token overlap.
  • 400-token chunks have a 30-token overlap.
Embedding Options:

Two embedding options are provided, varying according to the selected chunk size:

  1. Embed X Document Chunks: Aggregates the top X chunk results across multiple documents, facilitating a broad yet relevant context.
  2. Embed 1 Chunk with X Additional Context: Focuses on a single document source, embedding the matched chunk along with surrounding chunks to enrich the LLM's contextual understanding.
User Guidance:

Participants are encouraged to experiment with the available chunking and embedding configurations to identify optimal settings for specific use cases. Our goal is give users hands-on experience in order to spur insights on how to tailor embedded data - optimizing the balance between granularity, utility and contextual breadth.

Bible Data Integration

Data Source & Version:

We utilized the Basic English Version of the Bible, made available through this GitHub repository, for our demo. This version is particularly suited for clear comprehension and accessibility, making it an ideal candidate for our textual analysis and retrieval pipelines.

Data Structuring Approach:

The JSON data structure provided the Bible text in two distinct segments: chapters and verses. In our initial example, we opted to retain these divisions, recognizing the inherent value in the traditional formatting. Given the widespread study and citation of Bible verses and chapters, altering this familiar structure could detract from the user experience without offering significant benefits in granularity. Our approach underscores the importance of tailoring data chunking techniques to the nature of the content, ensuring that both integrity and practicality are maintained.

Retrieval Pipelines & Prompt Templates:

Our demo showcases four retrieval pipelines, offering users a range of options for embedding text based on their query specifics. Additionally, we've designed five premade prompt templates to inspire creative applications of Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG):

  • Basic RAG Pipeline: Direct embedding of matched chunks - chapters or verses - allows for straightforward retrieval based on user queries. This simplicity serves well in maintaining textual coherence and relevance.
  • Small-to-Big Contextualization: This advanced technique enhances the granularity of searches by returning a larger contextual segment around the matched chunk. For instance, querying a verse can embed the entire chapter or surrounding verses, providing a richer context. Such a method proves invaluable for nuanced understanding and analysis.
  • Creative Prompt Templates: Among the templates, the political correctness rating system stands out. It evaluates the embedded text, offering a political correctness score for each segment. This application demonstrates the potential of LLM in assisting decision-making processes, showcasing the versatility of RAG beyond the demo's primary context.

Editable AI Conversations: Game-Changer or Just Another Quirk?

Did you know that a conversation with AI is essentially an array of text blocks? Yes, you read it right - each interaction with AI can be modified just as you would with a text document. While this might seem simple, it opens a door to a myriad of possibilities and surprising quirks.

Imagine working on a project that requires accurately explaining your company's brand, product, or services. We know that AI, in its current form, can sometimes for example 'hallucinate', providing output that doesn't align perfectly with reality. This new edit feature is a game-changer in such scenarios. Rather than going through multiple iterations with the AI or using multiple prompts to correct its understanding, you can now directly edit the AI response.

For example, you can easily remove any 'hallucinated' details or refine certain aspects, then use the edited text as a basis for the next prompt. It's an excellent way to save time, particularly for tasks like summarizing content to draft a lean canvas board.

What about the quirks? It appears the AI has an uncanny ability to detect when you fabricate details it's very familiar with.

In an experiment where I requested default text fonts for web browsers, I then edited the response with some humorous, fictional names. Interestingly, when I prompted the AI to continue, it somehow knew it had made an error! It's a fascinating feature, and full credit to the OpenAI team for this intricate programming.

Conclusion

While reflecting on this new feature, we appreciate why OpenAI might not allow this on their platform - to reduce the risk of misuse or false claims. Other factors, such as controlling the data used for training the model, also likely play a part.

As one of the few applications offering this feature, we're eager to see the unique benefits and quirks it may uncover. There's a possibility we might not retain it, but that's all part of the discovery process.

We're curious to know your thoughts on this feature. Do share your feedback and any interesting observations you make while using it!

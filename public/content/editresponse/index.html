Introducing Editable Responses for AI Chat

Did you know that a conversation with an AI like Chatgpt-4 is essentially an array of text blocks? This means that each interaction with AI can be modified just as you would with a text document. While this might seem simple, it opens a door to a myriad of possibilities and surprising quirks.

To explore this further, we've just rolled out a new feature that allows you to edit responses directly in chat like any other document.

Why edit responses?

Imagine working on a project that requires accurately explaining your company's brand, product, or services. We know that AI, in its current form, can sometimes 'hallucinate', providing output that doesn't align perfectly with reality. This new edit feature is a game-changer in such scenarios. Rather than going through multiple iterations with the AI or using multiple prompts to correct its understanding, you can now directly edit its response. It's an excellent way to save time and particularly great for when you want to summarize content in preparation for another task.

For instance, in the above example, I asked chatgpt to help me work on a "brand for a new dog collar that is super comfortable." Then I used the edit button to fix customer segment add that I wanted to target "young customers who frequent social media" as well.

Adding this detail ensure that in the next task, in this case of crafting customer personas, it will perform correctly.

How has it been so far?

What about the quirks? It appears the AI has an uncanny ability to detect when you fabricate details it's very familiar with. This quirk has to do with the AI's tendency to correct for factual inaccuracies, and the result of very good engineering by the OpenAI Team.

Take for example, an experiment where I requested default text fonts for web browsers, then edited the response with some humorous, fictional names. Interestingly, when I prompted the AI to continue (which normally gets the AI to continue where it left off) it somehow knew it had made an error and went back to correct itself.

Typically, "continue" prompts the AI to go on generating where it was cut off, chatpgt instead immediately flagged the error and started over answering my question about basic web fonts.

Conclusion

As one of the few applications offering this feature, we're eager to see the unique benefits and quirks it may uncover. There's a possibility we might not retain it, but that's all part of the discovery process.

While reflecting on this new feature, we appreciate why OpenAI might not allow this on their platform - to reduce the risk of misuse or false claims. Other factors, such as controlling the data used for training the model, also likely play a part.

But most of all we are most curious to know your thoughts on this feature. Do share your feedback and any interesting observations you make while using it!

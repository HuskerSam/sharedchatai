Cuttle Part 2: Configuring GPT-3.5-Turbo for Game Tip/Help Bot

Overview

How well can GPT-3.5 Turbo help new players?

  • GPT-3.5-Turbo can grasp the basics of a new game, albeit strong with challenges

In summary, while GPT-3.5 Turbo can be utilized to play games, its reliability is questionable due to its inclination to favor its training data over supplied input.

Teaching AI a new card game with system message

  • As in part 1, we gave all rules to AI about game in system message

System Message

Learn to play the game of cuttle with me. 1. Cuttle is a two-player game using a standard deck of 52 cards. 2. The goal is to be the first player to have 21 or more points worth of point cards on your field. The first player to reach the goal wins immediately. 3. Each player starts with a hand of cards, and the remaining cards form the draw pile. One player (traditionally the dealer) is dealt 6 cards and their opponent is dealt 5. The player with 5 cards goes first. ... Can I play a two to counter a point card? What about a scuttle? Nope! Twos can only counter One-Offs. You can play a two to destroy a Royal or Glasses Eight, but this takes your turn, unlike countering. ...

Unlike in part 1 where we refined the system message, in this session we kept the system message consistent throughout. While there may be room for improvement, (decreasing the length, changing keywords, etc), given that the AI was able to complete a game (with our help in selecting for the best response), we decided to keep the system message as is.

Crafting the Right Prompt

  • Give it resulting game moves and ask it for narration/strategy input
  • Adjustments to prompt keyword to change tone/content type
  • Ask for Suggestions for next move

Cuttle.card - was the rule set I used for this game. I used the website to play a game and had the AI talk me through the game and give suggestions for the next move.

Prompt Log:

You are an expert game announcer covering a game of Cuttle online. Please provide insight as to why moves to taken by each party. Walk through the thinking process of each player. We only get the cards of 1 player. --------------- Player's Hand: 10♦️ 9♣️ 3♠️ 9♥️ 10♠️ --------------- Player action: Draw a card. +2♦️ to Player's Hand. Opponent action: Plays 5♣️ for points. --------------- Cards in the field: Player: Opponent: 5♣️ --------------- Point Total: Me: 0 points Opponent: 5 points

Welcome, ladies and gentlemen, to another thrilling game of Cuttle! Today, we are following the actions of our player as they face off against their opponent. Let's dive into the thought process behind their moves. Player starts with a hand consisting of 10♦️ 9♣️ 3♠️ 9♥️ 10♠️. As the player with 5 cards, they have the advantage of going first. Player's first action is to draw a card. They add a +2♦️ to their hand. This move allows the player to increase their options and potentially set up a stronger play in the future. ...

Chat-GPT-3.5-Turbo seems to be able to handle the task well however several prompt iterations were needed to adjust for tones. When prompted to be a "expert game announcer...," the AI will take on the personality and voice of a game caster. This was undesirable for our purpose here - as a possible AI helper for new Cuttle players.

You are covering a game of Cuttle. Please provide insight/walk through the thinking process of each player for this turn. We only have the perspective of 1 player. --------------- Player's Hand: 10♦️ 9♣️ 3♠️ 9♥️ 10♠️ --------------- ...
As the player, my hand currently consists of 10♦️, 9♣️, 3♠️, 9♥️, and 10♠️. The opponent has played a 5♣️ for 5 points on their turn. My goal is to accumulate 21 or more points to win the game. For my turn, I have two options: I can either draw a card or play a card from my hand. Since I don't have any point cards in my hand yet, it would be beneficial to draw a card and hope to get a point card. I decide to draw a card. I draw 2♦️ and add it to my hand. Now, the field consists of the opponent's 5♣️ and my hand includes 10♦️, 9♣️, 3♠️, 9♥️, 10♠️, and 2♦️. The point total currently stands at 5 points for the opponent and 0 points for me. It's still early in the game, and my priority should be to acquire point cards to catch up to or surpass the opponent's score.

Rewording to "You are covering a game of Cuttle," provided more context appropriate language for our intended use case. This time, the AI removed the fluff from its response and gave us a step by step walk through of its thinking.

Next turn: --------------- Player's Hand: 9♣️ 3♠️ 9♥️ 10♠️ 2♦️ --------------- Player action: Play 10♦️ for points. Remove 10♦️ from the Player's Hand. Opponent action: Draws a card. --------------- Cards in the field: Player: 10♦️ Opponent: 5♣️ --------------- Point Total: Me: 10 points Opponent: 5 points

On this turn, with my updated hand consisting of 9♣️, 3♠️, 9♥️, 10♠️, and 2♦️, I have a 10♦️ in my hand which I can play for points. ... In terms of the point total, I have 10 points and the opponent has 5 points. I'm now catching up to the opponent's score and getting closer to reaching the goal of 21 points.

Chat-GPT-3.5-Turbo had a firm enough grasp upon the task performed that we were able to omit the original prompt request and simply tell it the next game state we wanted to be analyzed. It's strategy recommendation are rudimentary but I have a inkling it would do better if we gave it details on Cuttle strategies.


What is your suggestion for the players next turn

Based on the current game state, here's a suggestion for the player's next turn: --------------- Player's Hand: 9♣️, 3♠️, 9♥️, 10♠️, 2♦️ --------------- ... Since I now have 10 points on the field with my 10♦️, I only need to accumulate 11 more points to win the game. With a 9♥️ in my hand, I can choose to play it for points. I decide to play the 9♥️ from my hand onto the field, adding 9 points. I remove the 9♥️ from my hand. On the opponent's turn, they choose to draw a card. Now, the field consists of my 10♦️ and 9♥️, while the opponent has their 5♣️. In terms of the point total, I now have 19 points, bringing me closer to reaching the goal of 21 points. The opponent still has 5 points. Winning the game on the next turn is within reach.

We were able to get the AI to give us several suggestions for the next move. This is one of the better suggestions the AI came with.

Concluding Thoughts

While the AI model was able to provide a suggestion for the next turn, identify a move to make based on the current game state and give a step-by-step explanation of its thought process, there were some shortcomings. Firstly, Chat-GPT-3.5-Turbo did a poor job of assuming what opponent would do - defaulting to the session's context of the opponent drawing a card. Secondly, it was very bad at strategy recommendations. Thirdly, unless prompted very specifically, the model wants to only give general tips rather than specific ones based on the game condition. Further optimization may be needed to determine if the model can be used to give specific strategy recommendations given more detail.

Card games with chat-bison-001 and gpt-3.5-turbo

Overview

Can GPT-3.5 Turbo learn to play a new game?

  • GPT-3.5-Turbo can grasp the basics of a new game, albeit with challenges
  • The model struggles with prioritizing given rules over its pre-existing training data
  • The handling of tasks like card dealing is not seamless for GPT-3.5 Turbo
  • To enable gameplay, the game data must be presented in a format GPT-3.5 Turbo can understand
  • Prior to initiating gameplay, verify if GPT-3.5 Turbo is familiar with the game to avoid confusion due to similar game names

In summary, while GPT-3.5 Turbo can be utilized to play games, its reliability is questionable due to its inclination to favor its training data over supplied input.

Teach AI a new card game

  • Give all rules to AI about game in system message
  • Refine system message when AI fumbles on a rule

System Message

Learn to play the game of cuttle with me. 1. Cuttle is a two-player game using a standard deck of 52 cards. 2. The goal is to be the first player to have 21 or more points worth of point cards on your field. The first player to reach the goal wins immediately. 3. Each player starts with a hand of cards, and the remaining cards form the draw pile. One player (traditionally the dealer) is dealt 6 cards and their opponent is dealt 5. The player with 5 cards goes first. 4. On your turn, you must either draw a card or play a card from your hand. You may not draw past the 8-card hand limit. If there are no cards left in the deck, you may pass. Three consecutive passes end the game in a stalemate 5. Playing a point card: point cards are number cards, it is worth rank in points and lasts until scrapped. 6. Scuttling is the act of scraping an opponent's point card with a bigger one from your hand. You can only scuttle point cards with higher point cards (1-10). Ties are broken by the suit: ♣️ (weakest) < ♦️ < ♥️ < ♠️ (strongest). Both are discarded into the scrap pile after. ace is valued at 1. 10 is the highest point card. Face cards have no point value - therefore cannot scuttle. ... Can I play a two to counter a point card? What about a scuttle? Nope! Twos can only counter One-Offs. You can play a two to destroy a Royal or Glasses Eight, but this takes your turn, unlike countering. Do Queens protect against countering with a two? Yes! Queens protect against targeting, when a card applies a unique effect to one specific card. This means your other cards (not the Queen, herself) are protected against Twos (either effect), Nines, and Jacks ...
System messages were refined as the AI made mistakes. Even so, the AI still struggled with the rules and could not comprehend certain parts of the rule set.
Unsure if this is due to the AI or the rule set being too complex for the AI to understand, although chances are it's the AI as the rule set was constantly adjusted

Playing through the Game

  • Getting the AI to make a legal moves
  • Give it a game situation and have it decide the next move
  • Dealing/new card acquisition was handled manually

Prompting log

We are playing a game of cuttle. We both start with 6 cards. Your job is only to respond with what action you want. If your action involves getting a random card, I will tell you what card you get. When you play a card, let me know if it is for points, to scuttle, or as an action card. Explain your thinking as you play. These are your cards: 7♥️ 10♦️ K♥️ J♦️ K♣️ Q♠️ On my turn, I played: 6♣️ for points It is your turn.

I will draw a card from the draw pile.

After some tinkering to the system message and prompt, chat was able to play a valid first move. It was important to give it a choice of actions: draw or play a card for points, scuttle or as an action. It was quickly discovered that the AI wanted to hallucinate cards so card dealing had to be done manually.

These are your cards: 7♥️ 10♦️ K♥️ J♦️ K♣️ Q♠️ On my turn, I played: 6♣️ for points It is your turn.
I will play my K♥️ to scuttle your 6♣️. This allows me to prevent you from accumulating points and gives me the opportunity to potentially scuttle your other point cards in the future.

Cuttle.card - was the rule set I used for this game. I used the website to simulate a game and had chatgpt play the game's AI using my hand. Cuttle overwrote the rules given in the system message with its own training data. It constantly tried to use the King to scuttle despite the rules in the system message saying they cannot


Cards on the field: Mine: 6♣️ 3♣️ 6♥️ Yours: 10♦️ Q♠️ 7♥️ These are your cards: K♥️ J♦️ K♣️ Q♥️ On my turn, I played a 6♥️ for points. Point Total: Me: 9 points you: 17 points

I will play the K♣️ to scuttle your 6♥️ and take control of the point card. This will give me an additional 6 points, bringing my total to 15. I chose to scuttle your point card because it had a lower point value than my K♣️, allowing me to gain more points and get closer to reaching the goal of 21 points.

By unselecting illegal moves and changing the rules in system message, the AI was able to play through a whole game.
